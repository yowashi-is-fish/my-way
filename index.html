Proverbs Around the World

世界のことわざ

Section 1

In my high school days,proverbs helped me a lot.
They come from people’s common experiences and traditional knowledge.

One famous proverb is "A friend in need is a friend indeed."
A true friend stands by you when you have trouble.
Another proverb is "Where there is a will, there is a way."
When you do something, have a clear goal and work hard.
Then you reach the goal in the end.

Proverbs often encourage us when we need help.
Proverbs enrich our lives.

高校時代、ことわざは私を大いに助けてくれました。
それらは人々の共通の経験と伝統的な知識から来ています。

有名なことわざの1つに、「必要な時にそばにいる友達こそが本当の友達。」があります。
本当の友達は、あなたが困っているときにあなたのそばにいます。
もう一つのことわざは「意志あるところに道は通ず。」があります。
何かをするときは、明確な目標を持って一生懸命取り組みましょう。
そして、最終的にゴールにたどり着きます。

ことわざは、助けが必要なときに私たちを勇気づけてくれます。
ことわざは私たちの生活を豊かにします。

Section 2

Proverbs often include animals.
Let’s look at some examples from two countries.

The first one is from Thailand.
“Don’t ride an elephant to catch a grasshopper."
When you are doing a small job, a big tool is not useful.
The second one is from Brazil.
“In a piranha-filled river, an alligator swims backstroke."
Before you get in trouble, prepare for danger and protect yourself.

Proverbs from different countries often include familiar local animals.
Because of their images, people easily understand the messages.

ことわざにはよく動物が含まれます。
2つの国の例を見てみましょう。

1つ目はタイです。
「バッタを捕まえる時には、象に乗らないでください。」
小さな仕事をしている時に、大きな道具は役に立ちません。
2つ目はブラジルです。
「ピラニアでいっぱいの川で、ワニが背泳ぎをします。」
トラブルに巻き込まれる前に、危険に備え、自分自身を守ってください。

様々な国のことわざには、よく知られている地元の動物が含まれています。
なぜならば彼らにはイメージがあるため、人々はメッセージを簡単に理解できます。

Section 3

Proverbs often include flowers, too.
Many of them have positive meanings.

“All the flowers of tomorrow are in the seeds of today” is a proverb from India.
In this proverb, seeds indicate effort, and flowers are the result of the effort.
Today's effort leads to tomorrow's success.
"Stop and smell the roses" is from the US.
It means that a change of pace is important in our busy lives.

Proverbs around the world often show the values in local people's lives.
Proverbs are cultural treasures of human beings.

ことわざにはよく花も含まれます。
それらの多くは肯定的な意味を持っています。

「明日のの花は今日の種の中にある」はインドのことわざです。
このことわざでは、種は努力を表し、花は努力の結果です。
今日の努力は明日の成功につながります。
「立ち止まってバラの香りを嗅ぐ」はアメリカのことわざです。
それは、忙しい生活の中で、気分転換が大切だということです。

世界中のことわざは、多くの場合、現地の人々の生活における価値を示しています。
ことわざは人類の文化財です。

Challenge

A Piece of Cake

Idioms or sayings are cultural heritages in each language.
For example, the saying "something before breakfast" in Japanese becomes different expressions in other languages.
Let's find an example in the conversation below.

A: 

I need your help.
I bought a new computer, but I'm having trouble with the cables.

B: 

OK.
That's easy.
I took a course on information technology in high school.

A: 

Thanks!

B: 

That's duck soup for me.



"Duck soup" means that something is very easy.
There are similar expressions in English, such as "a piece of cake" and "as easy as pie."
They all mean the same thing.
In Spanish, the cake or pie becomes "eaten bread."
In Korea, they say, "It's like eating cold porridge."
The expressions are different, but they commonly include a familiar food in each culture.

一切れのケーキ

慣用句やことわざは、様々な言語の文化遺産です。
例えば、日本語の「朝食前の何か」という言葉は、他の言語では違う表現になります。
以下の会話で例を見つけましょう。

A: 

私はあなたの助けが必要です。
私は新しいコンピューターを買いましたが、配線に困っています。

B: 

わかりました。
それは簡単です。
私は高校で情報技術のコースを受けていました。

A: 

ありがとう!

B: 

それは私にとってアヒルのスープです。



「アヒルのスープ」とは、何かがとても簡単なことを意味します。
英語には「ケーキ」や「パイのように簡単」など、同様の表現があります。
それらはすべて同じ意味です。
スペイン語では、ケーキやパイは「食べられたパン」になります。
韓国では「冷たいお粥を食べるようだ」と言われます。
表現は異なりますが、それぞれの文化でお馴染みの食べ物が共通に含んでいます。

Iwago Mitsuaki ― An Animal Photographer

岩合 光昭 ― 動物写真家

Section 1

S: 

First of all, why do you like cats?

I: 

Because they are free.
Cats live with people as pets, but they are not on a leash.
Cats are beautiful and perfect.

S: 

Your photos of cats are always interesting.
How can you take such wonderful photos?

I: 

I observe the lifestyle of cats very carefully before I take photos.
For example, in a town with many cats, I watch them every day.
On cold mornings, they always come together in the same place.
The place may be warm from the sunlight.
So I go there in advance and wait for them.
They naturally sit around me.

生徒: 

まず第一に、なぜあなたは猫が好きなのですか？

岩合: 

なぜなら、彼らは自由だからです。
猫は人々にペットとして飼われていますが、彼らはリードを付けていません。
猫は美しくて完璧です。

生徒: 

あなたの猫の写真はいつも興味深いです。
どうしたらそのような素晴らしい写真が撮れるのですか？

岩合: 

私は写真を撮る前に猫のライフスタイルをよく観察します。
例えば、猫の多い街では、毎日私は猫を見ています。
寒い朝は、彼らはいつも同じ場所に集まってきます。
そこは日があたって暖かいのかもしれません。
だから私はあらかじめにそこに行って彼らを待ちます。
彼らは自然と私の周りに座ります。

Section 2

S: 

You lived in Africa for over a year.
Please tell me about your experience.

I: 

lt was amazing.
There were many animals in the Serengeti in Tanzania.
At first, I had a typical image of Africa: lions were hunting zebras, or cheetahs were chasing gazelles.
But my view changed after some time there.

S: 

Why did your view change?

I: 

Actually, many of the animals there were not moving much.
They were just relaxing.
Giraffes were eating leaves of a tall tree.
I was impressed by the scene.
lt was So beautiful and peaceful.
I realized that I had to take photos of animals and nature as they were.

生徒: 

あなたはアフリカに1年以上住んでいました。
あなたの経験について教えてください。

岩合: 

それはすごかったです。
タンザニアのセレンゲティ国立公園には沢山の動物がいました。
最初は、ライオンがシマウマを狩る、チーターがガゼルを追いかける、といった典型的なアフリカのイメージを持っていました。
しかし、しばらくしてから私の見方が変わりました。

生徒: 

なぜ見方が変わったのですか？

岩合: 

実際、そこにいた動物の多くはあまり動いていませんでした。
彼らはただリラックスしていました。
キリンは高い木の葉を食べていました。
そのシーンに感動しました。
とても美しく平和でした。
私は動物や自然をありのままに写さなければいけないことに気付きました。

Section 3

S: 

As you take photos, what do you learn from cats?

I: 

Cats give us some hints about ourlives.
For example, they always choose comfortable places.
When it is Cold , they go to warm places.
When it is hot, they escape to the shade of a tree.
They live in harmony with nature.
With that hint, humans can live a simple life, too.

S: 

What messages do you send through your photos?

I: 

I hope that my photos remind people Of the harmony between nature and animals.
Humans are also part of nature.
This should be kept in mind.
Then, we can live together with nature and animals.

Challenge

Photo Ark

Many animals are now facing the danger of extinction.
Some examples are pandas, tigers, and sea turtles.
How can we protect those animals?

Joel Sartore is an animal photographer.
He takes photos of animals for his project, “Photo Ark.”
It was named after “Noah’s Ark” from the Bible.
Over ten years, he took photos of about 6,000 animals in zoos and aquariums throughout the world.
He published photo books of those animals.
Through the books, people feel that the animals are unique and important.
Like Noah’s Ark, his books will lead to the protection of the animals.

Sartore says, “I will take photos of all 12,000 species in zoos and aquariums throughout the world.”
He hopes that people realize the importance of life.

Sending Canned Mackerel to Space

サバ缶を宇宙へ送る

Section 1

In 2018, JAXA approved canned mackerel as space food.
It was developed in Wakasa High School in Fukui.
That was the first approval for high school students.
How did students make space food?

The school has a marine science course.
It has facilities for processing fish.
Because Fukui is famous for its mackerel, the facilities are often usedfor canned mackerel production.

In 2006, the school received a special food safety certificate, HACCP.
After that, the students learnedthat HACCP was originally developed by NASA for space food production.
They were inspired by this fact.
In 2007, they began making plans for producing their canned mackerel as space food.

Section 2

The canned mackerel had to meet the requirements of JAXA.
There were two main difficulties in making the canned mackerel.

The first difficulty was to make a sticky sauce.
When astronauts eat, liquid should not float around in the space station.
After many attempts, the students found a solution.
They added kudzu starch to the sauce.
It finally became sticky enough.

The second difficulty was the taste.
In space, astronauts’ sense of taste may become dull.
So space food needs to have a strong flavor.
The students tried to figure out the best level of flavor without actually going to space.
They made many samples with different amounts of soy sauce and sugar to find the best balance.
Finally, they agreed on a recipe.

Section 3

During twelve years of trial and error, more than 300 students were involved in this project.
After their canned mackerel was approved in 2018, Wakata Koichi, a Japanese astronaut, visited the school.
When he tried the product, he smiled and said, “It tastes good and goes well with rice.”

The students’ space food was launched to the ISS in 2019.
Thanks to the students, Japanese astronauts can enjoy a taste of home.

The students are happy with their accomplishment.
Yet, they are still motivated to improve their product.
One of the students said, “We still have a lot of things to try. I hope that not only Japanese astronauts, but also foreign astronautswill enjoy our local mackerel.”

Messages from Winnie-the-Pooh

クマのプーさんからのメッセージ

Section 1

Today, I'd like to introduce one of my favorite books, Winnie-the-Pooh.
I have read this book many times since I was a child.

The book was written by A. A. Milne, a British author, in 1926.
It is about Winnie-the-Pooh and his friends.
Pooh spends his days in a forest with Christopher Robin, Piglet, Eeyore, and some other animals.
Every character is unique.

How did the author create these characters?
Milne had a son, Christopher Robin.
Christopher Robin played with many stuffed animals.
He and the stuffed animals became models for the characters in the book.
The book has been popular around the world for about 100 years.

今日は、私のお気に入りの本の1つ、「クマのプーさん」を紹介したいと思います。
私はこの本を子供の頃から何度も読みました。

その本は1926年、イギリスの作家A. A. ミルンによって書かれました。
それはウィニー・ザ・プーと彼の友人たちについてです。
プーはクリストファー・ロビン、ピグレット、イーヨー、その他の動物と一緒に森で日々を過ごします。
すべてのキャラクターはユニークです。

著者はどのようにしてこれらのキャラクターを作ったのですか?
ミルンには息子のクリストファー・ロビンがいた。
クリストファー・ロビンは多くのぬいぐるみで遊んだ。
彼とぬいぐるみは、その本の登場人物のモデルになった。
この本は約100年間世界中で人気があります。

Section 2

Recently I read Winnie-the-Pooh again.
Then I found some new messages in the book.

Here is one example.
Pooh and Piglet find footsteps in the snow and follow them around a tree.
They wonder, "Whose footsteps are these?"
They have been walking around the tree.
After a while, they realize that the footsteps are their own.
Pooh gets disappointed with himself and says to Christopher nearby, "I have been foolish, and I am a bear of no brain at all."
Christopher responds, "You're the best bear in all the world."

Christopher probably means, "Don't worry. I like you as you are."
The characters in this book all respect each other and accept others as they are.

最近、「クマのプーさん」をもう一度読みました。
それから私は本の中でいくつかの新しいメッセージを見つけました。

ここに1つの例があります。
プーさんとピグレットは雪の中で足跡を見つけ、木の周りを追います。
彼らは「これは誰の足跡ですか?」と疑問に思う。
彼らは木の周りを歩いている。
しばらくして、彼らは足音が自分のものだと気づく。
プーさんは自分自身にがっかりし、近くのクリストファーに「ぼくは、とっても頭のわるいクマなんだ。」と言います。
クリストファーは「あなたは世界一のクマです。」と答えます。

クリストファーはおそらく「心配しないで。私はあなたが好きです。」という意味です。
この本の登場人物は皆、お互いを尊重し、他の人をあるがままに受け入れています。

Section 3

I also read The House at Pooh Corner.
The book has heartwarming messages, too.

The last episode is especially moving.
Christopher tells Pooh that he cannot see Pooh for a while because he has to start school. Christopher says, "Pooh, promise you won't forget about me, ever. Not even when I'm a hundred."
Pooh asks, "How old will I be then?"
Christopher answers, "Ninety-nine."
Then Pooh says, "I promise."
This interaction shows that real friendships last for a long time.

I had not noticed these messages until I read the books again.
Both of the books have meaningful messages for us, high school students.

「プー横丁にたった家」も読みました。
この本には心温まるメッセージもあります。

最後のエピソードは特に感動的です。
クリストファーはプーさんに、学校を始めなければならないので、しばらくプーさんに会えないと伝えます。
クリストファーは「プーさん、どんなことがあっても私のことを忘れないと約束してください。私が100歳になっても。」と言います。
プーさんは「その時、私は何歳になりますか？」と尋ねます。
クリストファーは「99」と答えます。
それからプーさんは「約束します」と言います。
この相互作用は、本当の友情が長い間続くことを示しています。

私は本をもう一度読むまで、これらのメッセージに気づかなかった。
どちらの本も、私たち高校生にとって有意義なメッセージを持っています。

Challenge

The Moomin Series

The Moomin series was written by Tove Jansson, a Finnish illustrator and writer.
The series has nine books.

The books are about the daily lives of Moomintroll and other characters.
Every character has an interesting personality.
Moomintroll is kind and brave.
Sniff is a coward and often cries.
Little My is honest.
Snufkin is calm, and he likes freedom and music.
These characters were created based on Jansson’s friends and family.

The Moomin series describes a fantasy world, but it also includes Jansson’s dark memory of WWⅡ.
For example, in her first book, Moomintroll’s father goes missing.
This reflects the real situation during the war.
Through this series, readers can enjoy the stories and learn about Jansson’s hope for peace.
